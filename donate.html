The Donation Process

Donating blood is simple, safe, and takes less than an hour from start to finish.

Registration

Complete a donor registration form with your basic information.

Health Check

Quick health screening including blood pressure and hemoglobin test.

Donation

Actual donation takes about 8-10 minutes for whole blood.

Refreshment

Enjoy snacks and drinks while you rest for 10-15 minutes.

Before You Donate

  • Eat a healthy meal before your donation
  • Drink plenty of water or juice before and after
  • Bring your donor card or photo ID
  • Wear clothing with sleeves that can be rolled up

After You Donate

  • Drink extra fluids for the next 24-48 hours
  • Avoid strenuous physical activity for 5 hours
  • Keep the bandage on for at least 4 hours
  • Eat iron-rich foods to replenish iron stores

Are You Eligible to Donate?

Use our quick eligibility checker to see if you can donate blood today. Most people can donate if they're in good health and meet basic requirements.

Basic Requirements:

  • At least 17 years old (16 with parental consent)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • In good general health

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about blood donation

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). This waiting period allows your body to replenish the lost blood. Platelet donors can donate more frequently - up to 24 times per year.

You may feel a slight pinch when the needle is inserted, but the actual donation process shouldn't be painful. Most donors compare it to a mild discomfort similar to a routine blood test.

The entire process takes about an hour from start to finish. The actual blood donation only takes 8-10 minutes for whole blood donation. The rest of the time is spent on registration, health screening, and recovery.

Please bring a valid photo ID (driver's license, passport, or donor card). If you have a donor card from a previous donation, bring that as well. It's also helpful to bring a list of any medications you're taking.

No, you should wait until you're feeling well to donate blood. If you have a cold, flu, or other minor illness, please wait until you've fully recovered before scheduling your donation.
